Welcome To CPU Scheduling Simulator

GUIDELINES

1. First of all, select one out of the four scheduling algorithms by pressing the radio-button beside them.

2. After choosing the desired scheduling algorithm, enter the required value in ‘Execute Time’ column, you have to also enter the priority and time quantum for Priority and Round Robin scheduling respectively

3. By default, two rows have been entered along with all their necessary parameters. You may edit them to your desired inputs, by altering the necessary parameters.

4. You can also add or delete new rows, by clicking the plus or minus symbol beside the table.,

5. After adding all the necessary inputs to the table, click on ‘Go’ to generate the Gantt Chart.

6. The Timer below returns the total time taken for the execution.

Below mentioned are the definition of each of the column heading of the table:

Process ID: It depicts the process ID of each individual process.

Process Number: It depicts the serial number of each individual process.

Execute Time: The execution time or CPU time of a given process is defined as the time spent by the CPU executing that process.

Service Time: The amount of CPU time that a process will need before it voluntarily exits the CPU.
